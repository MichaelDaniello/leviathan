# LEVIATHAN — Character Creation

Welcome to the crew. You're either desperate, crazy, or both. Good. That's the job requirement.

---

## THE BASICS

**Name:** What do people call you? (And what do people who want you dead call you?)

**Callsign:** Optional. But you're on a ship. You'll get one whether you want it or not.

**Species:** Human is default. Alien species exist but are rare in core space. Talk to VEGA if you want to get weird with it.

**Age:** Old enough to regret things.

**Background:** Where you came from. Colony kid? Corporate drone gone rogue? Military deserter? Failed pirate? The universe is full of ways to end up on a salvage ship with no prospects.

**Role on the Leviathan:** What do you *do*? Everyone pulls weight or everyone dies. Pick one:
- **Pilot** — You fly the ship. Badly, sometimes, but you fly it.
- **Mechanic** — You keep the ship from exploding. No pressure.
- **Muscle** — You solve problems with violence. Direct. Effective.
- **Face** — You talk your way in and out of things. Useful until it isn't.
- **Medic** — You keep the crew alive. The ship's medbay is... limited.
- **Tech Specialist** — You hack, you scan, you break into things that want to stay closed.
- **Wildcard** — You don't fit a box. That's either an asset or a liability.

---

## STATS

You have **30 points** to distribute across six stats. Each stat starts at 1 (minimum) and can go up to 10 (maximum). Average human is 5.

| Stat | What It Does |
|------|--------------|
| **GRIT** | Physical toughness. How much punishment you take before you drop. |
| **REFLEX** | Speed, agility, reaction time. Dodging, shooting, not dying. |
| **TECH** | Technical aptitude. Hacking, repairs, using complex systems. |
| **WITS** | Mental sharpness. Noticing things, solving problems, not being fooled. |
| **NERVE** | Courage under pressure. Resisting fear, intimidation, cosmic horror. |
| **CHARM** | Social ability. Persuasion, deception, making friends (or pretending to). |

---

## THE ROLL SYSTEM

When the outcome is uncertain and stakes exist, you roll.

**Basic roll:** 2d6 + relevant stat modifier

| Stat Value | Modifier |
|------------|----------|
| 1-2 | -2 |
| 3-4 | -1 |
| 5-6 | +0 |
| 7-8 | +1 |
| 9-10 | +2 |

**Outcomes:**
- **12+** — Full success. You did the thing, maybe even better than expected.
- **10-11** — Success with a complication. You did it, but...
- **7-9** — Partial success. You got something, but there's a cost or a catch.
- **6 or less** — Failure. The universe reminds you who's in charge.

**Critical rolls:** Natural 12 = critical success (something extra good). Natural 2 = critical failure (something extra bad, probably funny).

## GEAR & INVENTORY

You start with:
- Basic clothing appropriate to your background
- One weapon (if your role makes sense for it)
- One tool of your trade
- A personal item with sentimental value
- Whatever's in your pockets when you woke up on the ship

The Leviathan has a shared armory, medbay, and cargo hold. Taking things without asking VEGA is possible but *noticed*.

---

## YOUR EDGE

Pick **one special ability** that makes you stand out. Work with VEGA to define it. Examples:
- "Ghost in the System" — Once per session, auto-succeed on a hacking attempt
- "Hard to Kill" — When you'd drop to 0 HP, stay at 1 instead (once per session)
- "Silver Tongue" — Reroll any failed CHARM check once per session
- "Combat Instincts" — Act first in combat, always
- "Lucky Bastard" — Once per session, reroll any dice roll

Make your own. Keep it thematic. Keep it roughly equal in power.

---

## THE QUESTION

Every character has a question that defines their arc. What's driving you? What do you need to resolve? This isn't mechanical — it's narrative. But VEGA will remember it.

Examples:
- "Who sold out my crew?"
- "Can I ever go home?"
- "Is there a line I won't cross?"
- "What happened to me in the blackout?"
