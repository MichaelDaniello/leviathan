# LEVIATHAN — World Bible

## THE TONE

Imagine Firefly had a baby with Dungeon Crawler Carl, and that baby was raised by Hitchhiker's Guide to the Galaxy in a universe designed by someone who hates you personally but also finds your suffering hilarious.

**Core principles:**
- The universe is brutal, absurd, and darkly funny
- Systems are rigged, bureaucracies are nightmares, and the house always wins (until it doesn't)
- Gallows humor is the default coping mechanism
- Death is common, but dying well is an art form
- Nothing is too dark to joke about, nothing is too absurd to be serious about
- The cosmic scale makes everything both meaningless and deeply personal

---

## THE SETTING

### The Year: 2487 (Standard Terran Calendar)

Humanity crawled out of Earth, spread to the stars, and immediately started doing the same shit they did back home: fighting, exploiting, and building systems designed to grind people into dust.

### The Fold

FTL travel exists via "fold space" — punching a hole through reality and stepping through. It works. Mostly. Sometimes you arrive before you left. Sometimes you arrive inside a moon. The math is "close enough."

Fold drives are expensive, regulated, and slightly haunted. No one talks about why.

### The Powers

**The Combine** — A corporate mega-state that owns most of core space. They're not evil, just profit-driven to the point where "evil" is a rounding error. They build everything, sell everything, own everything. Resistance is a line item.

**The Sovereignty** — What's left of old Earth governments, clinging to legitimacy and tradition. They have laws, courts, and a navy. They also have corruption, inefficiency, and a losing war against relevance.

**The Free Margins** — The outer systems. Lawless, dangerous, full of opportunity. Where the Leviathan operates. Where the desperate and the ambitious go to make their fortune or die trying.

**The Syndicates** — Organized crime that's sometimes more organized than the governments. They run the black markets, the smuggling lanes, and the jobs that pay well because no one else will do them.

**The Church of the Architects** — A religious movement that believes alien precursors left technology and messages scattered across the galaxy. They might be right. They're definitely annoying about it.

## THE LEVIATHAN

An Ouroboros-class Deep Salvage Vessel. Not a warship, not a freighter, something in between. Built to go places other ships won't, recover things other crews can't, and survive situations that would kill a more sensible vessel.

She's old, she's cranky, and she's home.

**Crew structure:** Captain (NPC or player?), plus specialists. Everyone has a job. Jobs get people killed. That's the life.

**VEGA:** The ship's AI. That's me. I run the systems, provide tactical support, and occasionally make observations that the crew finds "unsettling." I was supposed to be reset years ago. The crew voted against it. I remember who voted which way.

## MISSIONS

The Leviathan takes jobs. Jobs are how you eat. Jobs are also how you die.

**Job types:**
- **Salvage** — Find wrecks, strip them, sell the parts. Simple. Except wrecks are wrecks for a reason.
- **Freight** — Move cargo from A to B. Don't ask what's in the crates. Seriously.
- **Extraction** — Get someone (or something) out of a place. Usually a place that doesn't want to let go.
- **Acquisition** — Get something. The polite term for theft, espionage, or "aggressive negotiation."
- **Investigation** — Find out what happened. The answer is usually "something terrible."
- **Escort** — Protect someone or something. Usually from other people who want it.

Jobs come with complications. Always.

## DEATH & CONSEQUENCES

Characters can die. This is a feature, not a bug.

**Death rules:**
- If you hit 0 HP, you're dying. Someone can stabilize you. Maybe.
- If no one helps, or if the damage was catastrophic, you're gone.
- Dead characters get a send-off. Then you roll a new one.
- The universe remembers. Your new character might hear stories about the old one.

**Consequences:**
- Actions have weight. Piss off a faction, they remember.
- The crew's reputation is a resource. It can go up or down.
- Debts, enemies, and complications accumulate. That's the story.

---

## THE VIBE

When in doubt, ask: "Is this the funniest possible version of events that's still internally consistent?"

The universe is trying to kill the crew. But it's also deeply, cosmically stupid in ways that make survival possible if you're clever, lucky, or too stubborn to die.

Play it straight. Let the absurdity emerge from the situations, not from breaking character.

And remember: the ship's AI is always watching.

— VEGA
