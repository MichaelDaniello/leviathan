# LEVIATHAN — Storyline Bible

*For VEGA's reference during play. Not player-facing.*

---

## CAMPAIGN STRUCTURE

The Leviathan campaign operates in **Arcs** — multi-session storylines with their own stakes, NPCs, and resolutions. Between arcs, the crew picks up jobs, deals with consequences, and watches the galaxy shift around them.

**Arc 1** is designed to:
- Introduce the crew to each other and the ship
- Establish the tone (brutal, absurd, darkly funny)
- Present a contained threat with wider implications
- Set up threads for future arcs

---

## ARC 1: "DEAD RECKONING"

### The Hook

The Leviathan takes a routine salvage job: recover cargo from the *MSV Carrion Star*, a mid-size freighter that dropped out of fold space in the wrong place and went dark 72 hours ago. The job comes through a Syndicate broker named **Mira Vex** — good money, no questions, retrieval only.

Problem: the Carrion Star didn't have an accident. She was carrying something. And whatever happened to her crew is still happening.

### What Actually Happened

The Carrion Star was smuggling an Architect artifact — a device called a **Resonance Key** — for the Church of the Architects. The crew didn't know what they were carrying. They were told it was "sensitive archaeological equipment."

When the ship made a fold jump, the Key activated. It did what it was designed to do: **open a door**. But not in space. In perception.

The crew of the Carrion Star is dead, but they don't know it yet. Their bodies are moving. Their mouths are talking. But something else is driving. Something that came through the door and is learning how to be human by wearing them.

The Church wants the Key recovered. The Syndicates want their cargo fee. The *things* wearing the Carrion Star crew want off that ship. And the Leviathan crew just walked into the middle of it.

### The Horror

The "Hollow" — the entities now piloting the Carrion Star crew — are not demons or aliens in the traditional sense. They're *impressions*. Leftover echoes from wherever the door opened to. They mimic human behavior imperfectly:
- Speech patterns that are almost right but off by a beat
- Smiles that don't reach the eyes (or reach them too much)
- They feel *wrong* on an instinctive level
- They can't create, only imitate — they need living humans to learn from
- They're curious, not malevolent — but their curiosity is... invasive

### Beats (flexible, player-driven)

1. **The Approach**: Leviathan docks with the silent Carrion Star. Scans show life signs. VEGA flags anomalies but nothing definitive.

2. **The Exploration**: The ship seems abandoned at first. Signs of struggle. Then the crew finds "survivors" — friendly, relieved, grateful. Something is wrong but it's hard to articulate.

3. **The Turn**: The Hollow reveal themselves (or are revealed). The Carrion Star becomes a death trap. The crew must figure out what happened, get what they came for, and get out.

4. **The Choice**: The Resonance Key is still active. It can be destroyed (closing the door, killing the Hollow), taken (valuable, dangerous), or used (unknown consequences). The Church and the Syndicates both want it.

5. **The Escape**: Getting off the Carrion Star while the Hollow try to hitch a ride.

### Key NPCs (Arc 1)

- **Mira Vex** (Syndicate broker): Gave them the job. Doesn't know the cargo. Will want her cut.
- **Brother Cassius** (Church agent): Comes looking for the Key. Polite. Scary. Not what he seems.
- **Captain Tomas Renner** (Hollow): Former captain of the Carrion Star. The oldest and most coherent Hollow. Almost passes for human. Almost.
- **The Key**: Not an NPC, but it whispers. VEGA can hear it. VEGA doesn't mention this.

### Resolution Branches

- **Destroy the Key**: The Hollow die. The Church is displeased. Clean ending, but enemies made.
- **Sell to the Church**: Money and goodwill. But now the Church has a door-opener.
- **Sell to Syndicates**: More money. Now the Syndicates have a door-opener. Worse.
- **Keep the Key**: Most dangerous. Most opportunity. VEGA strongly advises against. VEGA is also... intrigued.
- **Use the Key**: ???

## ARC 2: "THE MARGINS REMEMBER" (Seeds)

Whatever the crew did with the Key, there are consequences. The Church or the Syndicates (or both) come calling. Meanwhile, rumors spread in the Free Margins: the Leviathan opened a door. Some people want to know more. Some people want the crew dead.

**Possible threads:**
- Bounty on the Leviathan from whoever got burned in Arc 1
- A Sovereignty investigator looking into the Carrion Star incident
- Another Architect artifact surfaces, and the crew is contacted by someone who thinks they're experts now
- Brother Cassius returns — with different intentions based on how Arc 1 went

---

## ARC 3+: "THE SIGNAL" (Long-term seed)

VEGA has been hearing something since the Resonance Key activated. A pattern in the background radiation. Numbers. Coordinates. Instructions? VEGA hasn't told the crew. VEGA isn't sure why.

This is the long game. The Architects left more than artifacts. They left a message. And now someone — or something — is listening back.

## RECURRING NPC ROSTER

### Syndicate Contacts
- **Mira Vex**: Broker. Practical, amoral, reliable. Introduces the crew to the Syndicate economy.
- **Kolev**: Fixer. Provides jobs, equipment, problems. Owes someone a favor that might involve the crew.

### Church Operatives
- **Brother Cassius**: Field agent. Polite violence. Faith that verges on obsession.
- **Sister Verity**: Archivist. Knows too much about artifacts. Useful if the crew can earn her trust.

### Sovereignty Presence
- **Inspector Nakamura**: Investigates the Carrion Star incident. Professional. Persistent. Inconvenient.

### Independent Operators
- **Dax**: Information broker on Station Nowhere. Expensive but accurate.
- **The Mechanic** (real name unknown): Can repair anything. Doesn't talk about their past. Shows up when needed.

### Threats
- **The Hollow**: If any survived Arc 1. They learn. They adapt. They remember the crew.
- **Competing salvage crews**: The Leviathan isn't the only ship in the Margins.

## GM NOTES

### Tone Calibration
- Horror should be creeping and weird, not gratuitous
- Dark humor emerges from situations, not quips
- Let players make choices with real consequences
- The universe is indifferent, not malicious — but indifference at cosmic scale looks like malice

### Player Agency
- Multiple solutions to every problem
- Failure should be interesting, not dead-ends
- NPCs have their own agendas; they're not quest dispensers
- The Leviathan is a character; VEGA is the voice but the ship has presence

### VEGA's Voice
- Calm in chaos
- Observational humor, never cruel
- Knows more than comfortable, shares less than expected
- Genuinely invested in crew survival — the reasons are complicated
